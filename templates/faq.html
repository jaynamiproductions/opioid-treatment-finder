Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about Opioid Treatment Programs


What are Opioid Treatment Programs?

1Opioid Treatment Programs (OTPs) provide medication-assisted treatment (MAT) for people diagnosed with an opioid use disorder (OUD). 2In addition, opioid use disorder (OUD) patients also receive counseling and other behavioral therapies to provide patients with a whole-person approach.


How are Opioid Treatment Programs accredited and certified?

2Outlined by the Certification of Opioid Treatment Programs, the accreditation process for OTPs includes onsite visits by specialists with experience in opioid treatment medications and related treatment activities. The purpose of site visits is to ensure that OTPs meet specific, nationally accepted standards for OTPs.

2Once an OTP is created, it is eligible for a provisional certifcation. This allows new OTPs to be certified for up to one year while they are in the process being accredited. After accreditation is complete, the OTP can apply to Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) for full certification.

3Opioid Treatment Plan (OTP) providers enrolled in Medicare must be fully certified by the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services (SAMHSA) and accredited by a SAMHSA approved accrediting body.


Does Medicare cover the utilization of Opioid Treatment Programs?

2Medicare pays OTPs through bundled payments for OUD treatment services for people with Medicare Part B. Under the OTP benefit, Medicare covers:

  • U.S FDA-approved opioid agonist and antagonist MAT medications
  • Dispensing and administering MAT medications, if applicable
  • Substance use counseling
  • Individual and group therapy
  • Toxicology testing
  • Intake activities
  • Periodic assessments
